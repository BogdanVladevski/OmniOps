# SOP-CCM-7.5 — Temperature Excursion Response for Vaccines

## Scope
Applies to lyophilised and liquid vaccines including Hepatitis B and BCG where cargo
temperature departs the WHO-recommended +2 °C to +8 °C cold-chain band during transport.

## Immediate actions (first 5 minutes)
1. Isolate the affected shipment and label it **DO NOT USE — under cold-chain review**.
2. Capture excursion start, peak/trough temperature, and total duration from telemetry.
   BCG and reconstituted vaccines are especially heat- and light-sensitive.
3. Distinguish a heat excursion from a freezing event — freezing irreversibly damages
   adjuvanted and liquid vaccines and is never recoverable.

## Escalation
- Contact the national immunisation programme cold-chain officer and the receiving
  facility. Provide the telemetry trace and vaccine vial monitor (VVM) status if present.
- Any freeze exposure for adjuvanted vaccine is an automatic discard — escalate immediately.

## Disposition
- Assess against the manufacturer heat-stability profile and VVM stage.
- Quarantine until the programme cold-chain officer issues a written use/discard decision.
- Record batch, VVM stage, duration out-of-range, and decision on the incident ticket.
